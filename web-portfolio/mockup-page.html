DRAFTING IDEAS

Usually I starting with a sketch of ideas I have for the flow of the website. It's generally messy and meant for me to just get ideas I have down so it may not be perfect looking nor anywhere near functional. Having a site map is also important at this stage since I can see easily, in a visual way, what my pages my website is made up of. Not only does this keep me unconfused, it keeps development easier. Linking a bunch of websites is already a chore, why not make it easier? Each box in a site with its own title indicates a button and where it goes. Basically, a map. Clever.

Sometimes if I'm feeling fancy, or just really want to have a good callback sheet while I'm developing, I'll use a "moodboard" which would have basic website things such as; buttons (on and off hover) designs, color schemes, fonts etc. In this example image my moodboard was created for a local, website-less, coffee shop.

Last, a greyed out prototype is made just so I can see the bare bones structure, this will let obvious unfriendly user experiences be known. While I won't catch every design fail this will act as a sift. Not only that, but the last thing I want is to be distracted by pretty colors and actual text and not Lorem Ipsum. After this bare bones prototype is made a full blown, color filled, mockup is finally made. Disbarring development, these are all the examples of how I have just come to start building sites.
